Our Story

A Legacy Rooted
130 Years Deep

Ma'he'na — meaning "my vegetable land" — has been in our family since 1895, now proudly tended by the fourth generation. What began as a simple tropical garden has grown into a thriving 8-acre estate, faithful to the original vision of nurturing the land with care and respect.

Coconut palms have towered here for generations, while spices, areca, and banana have flourished alongside. The land has fed our family, and now it shares its bounty with the world.

Beyond the Estate

Paddy Fields &
Our Creek-Side Garden

Along the estate's eastern boundary, a narrow water creek winds between our land and the golden rice paddies beyond. Ma'he'na holds a small parcel of this paddy expanse — transformed into a lush creek-side vegetable garden where traditional crops grow with their roots in rich, water-fed soil.

4 Natural Water Wells

Four ancient wells sit across the estate, naturally feeding its roots through every season. No bore pumps, no pipelines — just the earth giving what it always has, generation after generation since 1895.

8 Acres of Green

A remote, untouched expanse of 8 acres — alive with tropical biodiversity, far from the noise of the modern world, and shaped by 150 years of patient, loving stewardship.

How We Farm

Rooted in Tradition,
Grown with Care

Over 130 years and four generations, the Ma'he'na way of farming has never changed — no shortcuts, no chemicals, no compromise. Each season, we follow the land's own rhythm: planting by the rains, harvesting by hand, and returning what we take.

From the first crack of a king coconut to the careful stripping of cinnamon bark, every act is a continuation of a family tradition that stretches back to 1895.
